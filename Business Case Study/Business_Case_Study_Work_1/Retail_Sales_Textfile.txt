1. Project Summary

This analysis focuses on evaluating retail sales performance using a dataset of 500 transactions. The objective was to uncover meaningful business insights across different dimensions such as time, region, product, and sales channels.

Data processing and aggregation were performed using SQL, while an interactive dashboard was developed in Power BI to present the findings in a clear and visual format.

Key performance indicators (KPIs) analyzed include:

Total Revenue
Total Transactions
Total Customers
Average Order Value (AOV)

Additionally, trends and comparisons were explored to understand business performance from multiple perspectives.

2. Analytical Findings

Sales Trends:
Revenue remains consistent overall, with noticeable spikes during certain months, indicating seasonal buying behavior.

Regional Performance:
Sales distribution across regions is relatively uniform, suggesting a balanced market presence.

Product Contribution:
A limited number of products generate a significant share of total revenue, highlighting the importance of top-selling items.

Sales Channels:
Both online and offline channels contribute effectively, with online sales showing stronger performance during specific periods.

Category Insights:
Variations in category performance reflect evolving customer preferences and demand patterns.

3. Strategic Recommendations
Align marketing campaigns with high-demand periods to maximize sales impact.
Enhance digital marketing efforts to further boost online sales performance.
Implement customer retention strategies such as loyalty programs based on purchase behavior.
Reassess underperforming categories by optimizing pricing, promotions, or product mix.
Maintain continuous monitoring of KPIs through dashboards for better decision-making.
4. Final Remarks

The dashboard provides a comprehensive view of sales performance, enabling quick and data-driven decision-making. Consistent tracking of these insights will support long-term business growth and improved customer satisfaction.
